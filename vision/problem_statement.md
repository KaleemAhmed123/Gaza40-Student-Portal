Thousands of students affected by the crisis in Gaza possess strong academic potential and, in many cases, have already secured admissions or partial scholarships from international universities. However, obtaining a university offer alone is not enough for these students to safely continue their education abroad.

Most universities can only support students academically through scholarships or tuition waivers. They are often unable to assist with the operational and humanitarian challenges that come afterward, such as:

visa processing,
relocation coordination,
accommodation deposits,
living expenses,
travel arrangements,
documentation support,
university-specific onboarding,
and emergency financial gaps.

As a result, many highly capable students remain unable to relocate despite having admission opportunities.

At present, much of this process is handled manually through forms, spreadsheets, Notion databases, messaging groups, and ad hoc coordination between volunteers and administrators. This creates operational inefficiencies, delays urgent cases, causes fragmented communication, and makes it difficult to track student progress, funding requirements, and case ownership in a structured manner.

The organization aims to solve this problem by building a centralized humanitarian education support platform that streamlines the entire student relocation and support process.

The platform is specifically intended to support students currently located in Gaza who are affected by the ongoing humanitarian crisis and are seeking safe educational opportunities abroad. Since many students operate in difficult connectivity conditions, the system should remain accessible, lightweight, and usable across both mobile and desktop environments.

To ensure accessibility for a broader student audience, the platform should support both English and Arabic languages and act as a centralized and trusted operational space for students, volunteers, and administrators.

Core Problem

There is currently no unified system that can effectively manage the end-to-end journey of crisis-affected students seeking international education opportunities and evacuation support.

The organization requires a platform that can:

centralize student information,
manage university offers and scholarship data,
coordinate volunteers and regional administrators,
track funding gaps,
support visa and relocation workflows,
manage student queries and escalations,
and provide operational visibility across all active student cases.

Without such a system:

urgent students may be delayed,
important documents may be lost or overlooked,
financial needs may be inaccurately assessed,
volunteers may work without coordination,
and administrators may struggle to prioritize the most critical cases.
Vision

The goal is to build a humanitarian student case management platform that helps crisis-affected students transition from conflict zones into safe educational opportunities abroad as quickly and efficiently as possible.

The platform should serve as a centralized operational hub for:

students,
volunteers,
regional administrators,
and organizational leadership.

The system should not only manage applications, but also actively support the operational journey of each student until they are successfully relocated and enrolled.

What the Platform Must Solve
1. Student Registration & Profile Management

Students should be able to:

create profiles,
submit academic and personal information,
upload required documents,
indicate financial circumstances,
and track their application status.

The system must support both:

students who already possess university offers,
and students who are still seeking admissions support.

This distinction is important because students with confirmed offers may require urgent evacuation and funding assistance, while students without offers may require mentorship, application support, and university matching.

Due to the sensitive nature of student information, relocation support, and humanitarian coordination, the platform must support secure document handling and consent-based data collection practices throughout the onboarding and support process.

2. University Offer & Scholarship Tracking

The platform must allow students to submit:

university offers,
scholarship details,
tuition costs,
accommodation requirements,
living expenses,
visa-related expenses,
and any existing personal or sponsor contributions.

The system should calculate:

remaining tuition obligations,
estimated relocation costs,
and total funding gaps requiring organizational support.

This enables the organization to quickly identify:

fully funded students,
partially funded students,
and high-priority emergency funding cases.
3. Regional Coordination & Visa Support

Regional administrators are responsible for handling country-specific operational processes such as:

visa guidance,
immigration requirements,
accommodation coordination,
country-specific compliance,
arrival preparation,
and local escalation management.

For example:

a UK regional admin may handle UK visa and relocation processes,
while another regional admin may focus on Scotland or other regions.

The system must support regional ownership of student cases and allow administrators to manage students relevant to their operational area.

Student profiles, submitted opportunities, and support requests may require verification and coordination between administrators, regional teams, and volunteers before operational assistance and relocation support can proceed.

4. Volunteer & University-Level Support

Volunteers may include:

students,
mentors,
alumni,
professors,
or university representatives.

These volunteers primarily help students with:

university-specific onboarding,
admissions communication,
application completion,
interview preparation,
academic guidance,
and local student support.

The platform must allow volunteers to collaborate with assigned students while maintaining clear operational boundaries and permissions.

5. Funding Gap Identification

A major operational challenge is that many students receive partial scholarships that do not fully cover:

tuition,
accommodation,
living costs,
deposits,
travel,
or emergency relocation expenses.

The organization operates funding initiatives to help bridge these gaps and has already raised significant funds to support affected students.

The platform must therefore provide:

transparent funding calculations,
visibility into unmet financial needs,
and prioritization tools for urgent funding cases.
6. Case Workflow & Escalation Management

Each student should be treated as an active case progressing through multiple stages, such as:

profile submission,
document verification,
university offer review,
volunteer assignment,
funding assessment,
visa processing,
relocation support,
and final enrollment.

The system must provide structured workflows, status tracking, ownership assignment, and escalation management to ensure no student case is overlooked.

7. Student Query & Support Management

Students may encounter issues related to:

visas,
documentation,
universities,
funding,
accommodation,
travel,
or communication delays.

The platform must include a query/ticket management system where:

students can raise issues,
responsible teams can respond,
and cases can be escalated to appropriate volunteers or regional admins.
8. Centralized Communication & Announcements

The organization also requires a centralized communication channel where it can publish:

scholarship opportunities,
funding programs,
urgent announcements,
visa updates,
and relocation guidance.

This ensures students receive timely and verified information from a single trusted platform.

Beyond operational coordination, the platform should also function as a centralized knowledge and communication hub where students can access scholarship opportunities, university guidance, deadlines, visa-related updates, and other critical educational resources relevant to their relocation journey.

Expected Outcome

The proposed platform should transform a fragmented manual process into a structured, scalable, and transparent humanitarian operations system.

The system should help the organization:

support more students efficiently,
reduce operational delays,
prioritize urgent evacuations,
coordinate global volunteers,
improve funding transparency,
and ultimately increase the number of students successfully relocated into safe educational opportunities.

The platform is not simply an admissions portal; it is a humanitarian coordination system designed to bridge the gap between university opportunity and real-world student relocation.
